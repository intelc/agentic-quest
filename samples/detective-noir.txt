The Last Honest Detective

The rain hadn't stopped in three days. Detective Lena Vasquez watched it streak down the window of her office on the fourth floor of the Greystone Building, turning the city into a watercolor of neon and shadow.

The phone rang. It always rang at the wrong time.

"Vasquez Investigations."

"Detective, this is Margaret Chen. My husband is dead, and I don't believe the police report."

Lena reached for her notebook. "What does the report say?"

"Suicide. They found him in his office at Meridian Corp, thirty-second floor. Gun in his hand." A pause. "David was left-handed. The gun was in his right."

Twenty minutes later, Lena was in the lobby of Meridian Corp, the largest pharmaceutical company on the eastern seaboard. The security guard — a thick-necked man named Riggs — studied her PI license like it was written in code.

"Thirty-second floor's off limits. Police orders."

"I'm not going to thirty-two. I'm going to thirty-one. David Chen's assistant — is she still in the building?"

Riggs checked his log. "Sophie Park. Yeah, she's upstairs. Been here since six AM. Hasn't left in two days, actually."

The elevator was mirrored on all sides. Lena caught her reflection — tired eyes, coffee-stained collar, the slight bulge of the .38 under her jacket. Some detective.

Floor thirty-one was open-plan, humming with fluorescent light. Most desks were empty — it was past nine PM — but one was occupied. A young woman with dark circles under her eyes was typing furiously, surrounded by stacks of paper.

Sophie Park looked up. "You're not from Meridian."

"No. Margaret Chen sent me."

Sophie's eyes went wide. She glanced at the security camera in the corner, then back at Lena. "Not here," she whispered. "There's a coffee shop on Birch Street. Meet me in twenty minutes. And Detective — bring a flash drive. A big one."
