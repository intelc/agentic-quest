The Hollow Crown Tavern

Rain hammered the cobblestones of Ashwick as Maren pushed through the tavern door. The Hollow Crown was busy tonight — merchants from the northern caravans huddled over bowls of stew, a bard tuned a lute by the fireplace, and in the far corner, a hooded figure sat alone, nursing a drink that glowed faintly blue.

"You're late," said Bram, the barkeep, without looking up. He was polishing a glass that would never truly be clean. "The Guild posted a new contract. Something in the old mines."

Maren slid onto a stool. "What kind of something?"

"The kind that ate two scouts and sent a third running back missing an arm." Bram set down the glass. "Pays three hundred gold. Plus whatever you pull out of the mine itself."

The contract board hung by the door, covered in overlapping parchments:

- WANTED: Source of disappearances near Miller's Crossing (50g)
- ESCORT NEEDED: Merchant caravan to Dunhollow (120g, danger pay)
- URGENT: Clear the old silver mine of unknown creature (300g)
- MISSING: Elara Thorne, herbalist, last seen heading into Blackwood Forest

Maren's eyes lingered on the last one. Elara had sold her a healing salve two weeks ago. Good salve. Good woman.

The hooded figure in the corner stood and walked toward her. As they passed under the lantern light, Maren caught a glimpse of pointed ears and silver-threaded hair.

"You're the one they call Pathfinder," the stranger said. It wasn't a question. "I have a proposition that pays considerably more than anything on that board. But it requires... discretion."
