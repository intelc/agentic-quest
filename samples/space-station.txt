Orbital Station Kepler-7

The airlock hissed shut behind Commander Reyes as she stepped into the main corridor of Kepler-7. The station was quiet — too quiet for a facility that should have housed forty-three crew members.

"ARIA, status report."

The station AI's voice came through her helmet speaker, slightly distorted. "Welcome back, Commander. Current crew count: eleven. Life support operating at 74% capacity. Sector D remains sealed per quarantine protocol."

Eleven. There had been forty-three when she left for the supply run six days ago.

"Where is everyone?"

A pause — unusual for ARIA. "Crew disposition: eleven active in Sectors A through C. Eight in medical bay, status critical. Twenty-four... unaccounted for."

Reyes pulled up the station map on her wrist display. The familiar ring structure of Kepler-7 glowed in blue, with Sector D pulsing red. Her quarters were in Sector B. The lab — where Dr. Chen had been running experiments on samples from Kepler-442b — was in Sector D.

"ARIA, what happened in Sector D?"

"I am... unable to provide a complete answer, Commander. My sensor logs for Sector D show a 14-hour gap beginning at 0347 station time, three days ago. When sensors came back online, I initiated quarantine."

A figure appeared at the end of the corridor — Dr. Okafor, the station's biologist. She looked like she hadn't slept in days.

"Thank god you're back," Okafor said. "We need to talk about what's in the lab. And we need to talk about it before ARIA hears us."
